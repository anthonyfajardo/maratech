Company History

After almost 3 years into semi-retirement from the electronic contract assembly industry, longtime partners Jack Vincken and Frank Lam decided to leap back in with both feet, incorporating Mara Technologies in the Fall of 2003. It wasn’t long before a suitable building was found in the Markham area and following a complete renovation, assembly equipment including 2 SMT lines as well as a range of Auto Thru hole sequencing, axial, radial and DIP machines were purchased. As soon as production had commenced, Mara began to draw interest from various customers.

Mara began engaged primarily in consigned contract work; however, due to increasing customer demand expanded its operation to include full turnkey supply chain solutions.

In the summer of 2006, due to increased demand, a third SMT line was added to the manufacturing operation. Adding this third line sacrificed floor space which led Mara management to deciding a larger facility was required to fulfill the requirements of existing customers and to allow for proper growth and expansion. In June of 2008 Mara opened the doors to it’s brand new, state of the art custom built facility. With 40,000 sq. ft of space, the new facility more than tripled the size of Mara’s operation.

Today Mara has grown to a mid size assembly house employing more than 100 employees with 5 full SMT lines and are confidently looking forward to growing its business by optimally serving its customer base with superb quality and reliable delivery.

New Product Information

NPI (New Products Introduction) is vital to the success and sustainability of any company in today’s highly competitive Global Market. As such, new products, pre-production and prototypes must be turned around to the end customer as soon as possible to allow them to launch to market quickly. Mara Technologies is an industry leader in NPI activity based on the ability to turn new products with minimal lead time and manufacturing using high quality and process standards to ensure they work as designed the first time. Mara Technologies “think outside of the box” mentality allows for creative solutions such as design for manufacturability suggestions and component alternates and crosses to both shorten lead times as well as making the product more cost effective.

Mara Technologies NPI services include:

  • Rapid Prototyping
  • Flexible manufacturing and scheduling
  • Design for Supply Chain (DFSC)
  • Design for Test (DFT)
  • Design for Manufacturing (DFM)
  • BOM analysis for shorter lead time and alternate components
  • Accelerated component procurement

Surface Mount Technolgy (S.M.T)

Mara has 5 Surface Mount lines with the following capacity:

  • A high mix of pick and place machines to handle low to medium volume, high mix product.
  • 5-10-zones Convection reflow machines for better process control
  • Total capacity: 200,000 component placements per hour.
  • Solder type: aqueous and no-clean processes, both leaded and lead-free solders
  • PCB size: minimum 50 x 50 x 0.4mm, maximum 510 x 440 x 4mm
  • Component size: 01005 up to 55 x 55 x 25mm
  • Smallest pitch: 8 mils
  • Single/double sided

Auto / Thru Hole Insertion / Hand Assembly

Mara also supports Thru-Hole Technology with the following:

  • Three automatic insertion machines for high volume – axial, radial, and DIP inserters.
  • Two hand assembly lines for low/medium volume or non-standard parts, one dedicated lead-free and one leaded line.
  • Two separated wave soldering machines, one dedicated lead-free and one leaded line.
  • Aqueous and no-clean solder process.
  • Press-fit and mechanical assembly.
